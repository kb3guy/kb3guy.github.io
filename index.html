Controls, Electrical, and Electronic work

VFD Accessory Box

This VFD accessory box was created to allow the operation of 220V three-phase, single-motor devices up to 1HP off of 120V power. It allows for multiple sources of frequency control input, has a port on the back for RS485 control, as well as the ability to manually adjust and monitor speed via a potentiometer and analog meter on the front control panel.

Last updated 6/14/2020

Hardinge Lathe Control Panel

This current project is a rebuild of a Hardinge HLV lathe control panel. The machine is being converted from running on supplied 440V three-phase power to 220V single-phase power using a VFD. This necessitates the use of a microcontroller to tell the VFD when it is safe to activate the motor, and to switch several contactors which control the connection of the different windings on the two-speed motor. The control panel makes use of the original barrel switch to provide the intended operational feel.

Mechanical Design

Shop Gantry Crane

I put together a number of designs for a shop gantry crane for use in machine restoration, and did some studies on the strength of materials involved and the joints necessary for supporting the expected load of one ton. I settled on and built a design using one free-standing end, and one end traveling on a track supported by a timber structure. The crane has successfully hoisted 125% of rated load by lifting the Van Norman mill (which weighs in at 2500lb.)

Machine Restoration

Pratt and Whitney Lathe

This small lathe came in excellent condition and required little except the fabrication of a countershaft unit to get started. It uses an unusual mechanism for rapid, repeatable threading, and has succesfully been used for small turning, drilling, boring, and threading operations in the shop. For its size, it is remarkably rigid and accurate, and is a joy to operate (despite being entirely OSHA non-compliant.)

Last updated 6/14/2020

Van Norman 16

The Van-Norman 16 mill is a previous restoration project. It uses a 2HP 220V three-phase motor to drive a geared head, and a 1/4hp three-phase motor to drive the table feed. The mill required fitting with two VFD's, some repair work to the table drive and horizontal arbor support, and a great deal of de-rusting. Custom milling arbors had to be designed and fabricated for the machine due to its use of an obsolete, proprietary collet system. It has been successfully used for small milling jobs, including gear-making.

Last updated 6/14/2020

HLV Lathe

This hardinge HLV lathe is a current restoration project, was previously owned by the Douglas aircraft company, and likely manufactured sometime between the late 1940's and 1959, when the model was made obsolete. It uses a 1HP, two-speed 440V three-phase motor. VFD and step-up transformer have been successfully tested to drive the motor, and the control panel is ready to be mounted and tested in-situ. Jobs to be completed include manufacture of an idler gear for driving the threading box, repair of the tailstock feed nut, accuracy testing, bed regrinding if necessary, and painting.

Last updated 6/14/2020

Hardinge UM Mill

This small mill by Hardinge is a current restoration project. It has a 3/4HP, two-speed 200V three-phase motor, and will require a microcontroller-switched interface to be operated properly with a VFD, similar to the one in development for Hardinge lathe. With its universal table, it is capable of helical milling. The machine is currently operable using a Bridgeport M-head driven by the accessory VFD box. Jobs to be completed include control design, scraping in, and painting.
